Union leaders explained to the participants the necessity of starting a small business primarily to go up in social and economic status. An expert, Mr shared his experience and knowledge in the field of micro, small and medium businesses. He gave them the details on the kinds of business that are feasible, the available financial support from government and other institutions and the necessary organisational structure to develop any business venture. Later about 23 of them volunteered to immediately apply for loans from banks and register with the government MSME (Micro, small and medium enterprises).

These trainings were held in the months of July and August before the Gram Sabha meeting held on 15 Aug 2024 throughout the state. The training consisted the following elements:

  • Identifying general and specific common needs of the community in the particular panchayat

  • Verifying the budgeted activities of the panchayat through E Gram Seva app and collect necessary information

  • Prepare a written petitions demanding necessary action for budgeted activities and other fresh needs to be added

  • Learning the art of speaking while participating in the Gram Sabha meetings

Later on 15 August 24, hundreds of women workers from nearly 190 villages participated in the Gram Sabha meetings of Dindigul, Virudhunagar and Madurai districts and put pressure on the panchayat officials to implement the budgeted activities.
